Australian Academy of Science

Elizabeth and Frederick White Research Conference on


Kaba Kada: Exploring the first billion years of the Universe

Oaks Port Douglas Resort, Queensland Australia

22nd - 26th September

Rationale

Submit an Abstract

In recent years, the field of astrophysics has made remarkable strides in our understanding of the universe's first billion years. The advent of powerful space telescopes like the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), combined with cutting-edge ground-based observatories such as the Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA) and the Hydrogen Epoch of Reionization Array (HERA), has revolutionized our ability to study the early universe in unprecedented detail. These advancements have provided profound insights into the formation of the first galaxies, stars, and cosmic structures, opening new avenues for exploration.

This conference aims to convene leading experts from around the globe to delve into these groundbreaking discoveries and chart the future trajectory of research in the field. As we stand on the brink of the next generation of observational tools, including the Square Kilometre Array (SKA), this event represents a pivotal opportunity to integrate and advance our collective knowledge of the early cosmos.

Key topics

  1. Observing Cosmic Reionization of Hydrogen

  2. Galaxy Evolution from the Cosmic Dawn

  3. Universe in Boxes

  4. Future Developments and Synergies

Venue

Six years after ‘Barefoot Reionization,’ we invite you to return to the heart of Tropical North Queensland in Australia— Port Douglas — where the wonders of both the cosmos and nature converge.

From here, the Great Barrier Reef, one of the world’s most breathtaking marine ecosystems, is easily accessible. Just a short drive north lies the world’s oldest tropical rainforest, the Daintree Rainforest or Kaba Kada which means the rainy place. We hope you enjoy the conference and take the opportunity to immerse yourself in these incredible landscapes, experiencing firsthand the natural and cultural treasures of this remarkable region.

The conference will be hosted at Oaks Port Douglas Resort.

Important Dates

  • Feb 17, 2025:  abstract submission begins. 

    To apply for a talk or poster, or to attend with no presentation, please click the “submit an abstract” button and fill the form before the due date. The conference is expected to host ~75 participants.

  • Mar 31, 2025:  abstract submission closes.

    All applicants will be notified of their results within 3 weeks after the submission closes. Presenters will be asked to confirm their participation and complete their formal registration for the conference.

  • May 12, 2025:  registration closes.

    Registration fees are 500 AUD, which covers bus between Cairns Airport and the venue, morning, afternoon tea and lunch each day, an opening reception on Sunday and a conference dinner on Thursday.

  • May 19, 2025:  program releases.

    Participants are encouraged to finalize bookings of their accommodations, transportations and recreational activities at this point. The venue page contains some useful information.

  • Sep 21, 2025:  welcome reception.

  • Sep 22, 2025: program begins.

  • Sep 26, 2025: program closes after lunch.
